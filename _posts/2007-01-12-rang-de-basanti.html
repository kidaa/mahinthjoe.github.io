Many of us may not approve of the glycerine tears and tomato ketchup or the frenzied running around trees, the white saree drenched in artificial rain or the rising cresendo of a hundred violins. - Film Critic Derek Bose in his new book 'Brand Bollywood'

Director Rakeysh Omprakash Mehra's highly acclaimed 'Rang De Basanti'

Starring Aamir Khan, R. Madhavan and Soha Ali Khan, the film, about the awakening of the youth, is also India's official entry to the Oscars.
